### Phase 1: Pre-Production Planning

#### Step 1: Song Selection & Analysis
- [ ] Choose or create base music track
- [ ] Analyze BPM and beat structure
- [ ] Identify verse, chorus, bridge sections
- [ ] Count available beats for character names
- [ ] Plan rhythm pattern for name chants

#### Step 2: Story Development
- [ ] Select story template
- [ ] Choose 8-15 characters for video
- [ ] Divide characters by role (hero/villain/support)
- [ ] Plan character arcs through video
- [ ] Design subtle narrative progression

#### Step 3: Environment Design
- [ ] Choose setting matching story
  - Urban battlefield
  - Industrial facility
  - Futuristic city
  - Underground bunker
  - Space station
  - Fantasy realm
  - Apocalyptic wasteland
- [ ] Plan lighting mood (dark, bright, neon, etc.)
- [ ] Design color palette
- [ ] Determine camera style (dynamic, static, cinematic)

#### Step 4: Shot List Creation
- [ ] Break song into 15-30 second segments
- [ ] Assign character introductions to beats
- [ ] Plan action sequences between names
- [ ] Design transition shots
- [ ] Plan climax visual peak

## ðŸŽµ Song Structure Integration

### Mapping Story to Music

#### Verse 1 (Usually 20-30 seconds)
**Story Purpose**: Introduction and setup
- **Visuals**: Environment establishment
- **Characters**: 2-3 main characters introduced
- **Pace**: Slower, clear character showcases
- **Lyrics**: Individual name chants with pauses

**Shot Breakdown**:
1. Wide establishing shot (3-4 seconds)
2. Character 1 entrance (3-4 seconds)
3. Character 1 showcase pose (2-3 seconds)
4. Character 2 entrance (3-4 seconds)
5. Character 2 showcase pose (2-3 seconds)
6. Both characters acknowledgment (3-4 seconds)

#### Chorus (Usually 15-20 seconds)
**Story Purpose**: Energy peak, main message
- **Visuals**: Rapid character cuts
- **Characters**: All main cast rapid sequence
- **Pace**: Fast, high energy
- **Lyrics**: Rapid-fire name chanting

**Shot Breakdown**:
1-10. Quick character flashes (1-2 seconds each)
11. Group shot all together (2-3 seconds)

#### Verse 2 (Usually 20-30 seconds)
**Story Purpose**: Development, opposition introduced
- **Visuals**: Opposing force showcase
- **Characters**: Antagonist team introduction
- **Pace**: Similar to Verse 1
- **Lyrics**: Villain name chants

**Shot Breakdown**:
1. Villain environment (3-4 seconds)
2-6. Individual villain showcases (3-4 seconds each)
7. Villain group menacing shot (3-4 seconds)

#### Bridge (Usually 10-15 seconds)
**Story Purpose**: Turning point, tension peak
- **Visuals**: Confrontation begins
- **Characters**: Heroes and villains face off
- **Pace**: Building tension
- **Lyrics**: Slower, dramatic name calls or instrumental

**Shot Breakdown**:
1. Split screen heroes vs villains (2-3 seconds)
2. Close-ups of key characters (1-2 seconds each)
3. Wide shot of imminent clash (2-3 seconds)

#### Final Chorus (Usually 15-20 seconds)
**Story Purpose**: Climax, battle peak
- **Visuals**: Action sequence
- **Characters**: Combat interactions
- **Pace**: Maximum energy
- **Lyrics**: Full-speed name chants

**Shot Breakdown**:
1-12. Rapid action cuts (1-2 seconds each)
13. Freeze frame victory/cliffhanger (2-3 seconds)

- [ ] Generate main environment background

- [ ] Create each character introduction shot

## ðŸ’¡ Pro Tips & Best Practices

### Character Showcase Tips
1. **Iconic Poses**: Give each character a signature pose (Cappuccino Assassino's stealth crouch, Bombardiro Crocodilo's bomb-ready stance)
2. **Color Coding**: Assign specific colors to characters for quick recognition (Blueberrinni = purple-blue, Chimpanzini = brown-yellow)
3. **Power Scaling**: Show clear visual differences between regular and ultimate versions (standard Tung Tung Tung Sahur vs Ta Ta Ta Ta extended version)
4. **Personality Through Posture**: Confident characters stand tall (La Vaca Saturno), sneaky ones crouch (Bobrito Bandito), chaotic ones twist (Trippi Troppi)

### Music Sync Tips
1. **Impact on Beat**: Major visual moments (landings, hits) should sync exactly with drum hits
2. **Name Length Matters**: Match syllable count to available beats
3. **Build Energy**: Start slow and controlled, increase pace toward chorus
4. **Silence Power**: Use brief pauses in music for dramatic character reveals

### Visual Flow Tips
1. **Rule of Thirds**: Position characters at intersections for dynamic composition
2. **Eye Line Match**: Characters should look toward next shot's character
3. **Motion Direction**: Maintain consistent screen direction (heroes from left, villains from right)
4. **Scale Variety**: Mix wide, medium, and close shots to maintain interest
5. **Color Contrast**: Heroes in cool colors (blue), villains in warm (red/orange), or vice versa

### Story Clarity Tips
1. **Clear Teams**: Use consistent positioning, colors, or symbols to identify teams
2. **Progression**: Show clear beginning, middle, end even without dialogue
3. **Understandable Conflict**: Make it obvious who's fighting whom and why
4. **Satisfying Payoff**: Climax should feel earned and exciting
5. **Cliffhanger Option**: End with setup for part 2 to encourage series

### Production Efficiency Tips
1. **Batch Generation**: Generate all character references first, then all shots
2. **Template Reuse**: Save successful prompts as templates
3. **Variation Generators**: Use AI tools that swap elements while keeping composition
4. **Asset Library**: Build reusable environment and effect libraries
5. **Preview Renders**: Test with low-res before final high-quality generation

- **Rivalry Arc**: Two characters have personal conflict
